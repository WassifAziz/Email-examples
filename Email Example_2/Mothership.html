Cold weather can be seriously bad for our health and make you more likely to catch a winter illness that could become very serious. Especially if you are aged 65 or over, suffer from breathlessness, have lung disease or you may have another long term condition such as diabetes, heart or kidney disease, make sure you have everything you need to stay well this winter.

Here are some great tips to help you or someone you know or care for, who has a long term health condition, to stay well this winter:


Flu vaccinations are offered free* of charge if you're aged 65 or over, have a long term health condition or are a carer, so you are protected against catching flu and possibly developing serious complications.

This may help prevent colds, flu or more serious health conditions such as heart attacks, strokes and pneumonia.

If you think you or someone you know or care for is coming down with a winter illness, act quickly. Your pharmacist is fully qualified to advise you on the best course of action.

Ask your pharmacist what medicines you should have in your cabinet to help get you and your family through the winter season. Make sure you are stocked up with enough food supplies for a few days and get any prescription medicines before the Christmas holidays start.

The weather might mean people are stuck indoors more than usual - ask your loved ones and people near you if they need any practical help.
